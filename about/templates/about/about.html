About Mike



Welcome to my website, Binge Traveller!
I hope that you find it insightful, informative, and entertaining!

Hi, I'm Mike Murtagh, and I am located in Dublin, Ireland

From as long as I can remember, I have always had an interest in geography. From sketching maps of Ireland, to flicking through the pages of an atlas. By Primary School, I knew the capitals of all the countries in the world, as well as rivers, mountains, lakes, and various cities. I knew the flags of each country, and dreamed of what it would be like to visit such far-flung destinations, and how it might be different to living in rural Ireland.

My Dad bought me a "bigger and better" atlas at a school book fair, and I literally never took my head out of this book!

At the same time, I discovered a passion for aviation and plane spotting, and so my parents bought me a pair of binoculars for Christmas when I was about 10 years old, so that I could plane-spot quite easily from my garden. I longed to be on those planes which were zooming at 40,000 feet over my head, and wondered where in the world they might be going to. My bedroom wall was covered in pictures of maps and photos of aeroplanes, from ceiling to floor.

I had written to various embassies in Ireland for further information about their mission and home country, as well as to airline offices for photos and pictures of their aircraft. There was also a globe of the world sitting on my bedside locker sitting on top of atlases and maps, inspiration from the moment I opened my eyes!

With my parents and grandparents, I visited Dublin Airport, Shannon Airport, and Knock Airport, and I immediately knew, "this was my place; this is where I belonged". I was completely amazed, not just by the buzz of the airport, but by the beauty of the aircraft up close, and seeing the huge departure board flicking with names of cities I had read about and looked-up in my atlas: Athens, Copenhagen, New York, Helsinki. Toronto.

Oh, what I would give to head off to any of these places! I had no sense of fear; I felt so independent, and just wished to go through those departure gates and jump on a plane.
Any plane!

It wasn't until I was 15 years old, that I got to take my first trip abroad and get to travel on a plane for the first time. I was in 4th year in secondary school, and our school had organised a trip to Barcelona. I begged my parents to allow me to go, and they agreed to let me go on this tour!
I don't think I was ever as excited in my life.

We flew on an old Ryanair Boeing 737-200 from Dublin (DUB) to London's Stansted (STN) airport, and from there with a coach to Dover. We crossed the English channel to Calais by ferry, and drove through the entire length of France via Troyes and Lyon down to Barcelona. We stopped in Paris on the return journey, and I was in awe of the Eiffel Tower and Arc de Triomphe. I took 3 rolls of film for my camera and snapped pictures of everything that I saw! I came back a changed person, as I had finally tasted what it was like to visit a new place. This trip lives long in my memory, and I even still have my old Ryanair boarding card from May 1996.

At this point, I had penpals in numerous different countries around the world. Egypt, Australia, Finland, Italy, Cyprus, Israel, Russia, Peru…. I was exchanging photos, cassette tapes of local music, stamps, currency, and learning more about life in other countries. It was also a fantastic way to brush up on my written French skills, and hence I had about 6 French penpals on the go at one time! I may have stolen money from my mothers purse in order to buy stamps at the Post Office, and I most definitely was caught by teachers many times in class at school writing letters to said penpals. All of which got me into trouble as you might imagine!

I then went into Leaving Cert with a renewed interest in Geography and French, and I decided to pursue my degree in Dublin City University (DCU) studying a Bachelors in Applied Computational Linguistics. I deliberately selected this course, as it allowed me to spend the third year of my program at a French university as part of the Erasmus exchange program. I eventually went to "UniversitÃ© de Provence I" in Aix-en-Provence, in the south of France, close to Marseille.

Being based there, I was able to visit amazing towns and cities throughout the south of France, and my love and thirst for travel and experiencing new things just grew and grew. I swam in the Mediterranean Sea at Plages du Prado in Marseille. I visited the incredible Palais des Papes in Avignon. I admired the stunning lavender fields near Salon de Provence. I attended the Monaco Grand Prix in Monte Carlo, and strolled on the Promenade des Anglais in Nice. I was literally in heaven there, and never wanted to leave.

Once I had reluctantly returned to Dublin and completed my degree, I vowed that I would travel the world and lead my life in a way that I get to experience as much culture, and meet as many new people, as possible. Over the years, I have discovered that I am a person who is not driven by material possessions; I'd much rather spend my money on experiences, and see the world with my own eyes. When I call my parents or friends now, they typically ask, "Where are you?", rather than, "How are you?" ðŸ˜‚

As a tradition, I always start my journey with a pint of beer at Dublin Airport (DUB), whether it is 5am, or 10pm. I have a few more on the plane (naturally!) just to pass the time, and I am always eager to try out local food and beer/wine/etc. at my destination.
As a result, the idea of "Binge Traveller" was born.

The aim is to provide inspiration to fellow solo travellers, as well as LGBTQ+ travellers who would like to gain an insight into cities or places which may be welcoming and inclusive. I am by no means a professional photographer or script writer, but I do hope that by documenting my personal experiences, and capturing some amateur photos along the way, that I can allow you to step outside of your comfort zone, and perhaps visit somewhere that you thought might be out-of-reach! All of my photos are taken by iPhone 11, or GoPro Hero 9.

Finally, I absolutely must say that I will be forever grateful to my parents, as they were the ones who instilled the love of travel in me from the very start. From buying me an atlas, binoculars, and allowing me to go on the school tour - these were the foundation stones that allowed me to discover the joy of travel, and which today is my sole passion in life.

Never Stop Dreaming, Push Yourself, and Fly High!

Big hugs,
- Mike
